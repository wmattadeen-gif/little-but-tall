# Little But Tall Charities Foundation Document

**Prepared for:** Board review and organizational alignment  
**Purpose:** Establish a clear source of truth for Little But Tall’s identity, mission, values, program direction, and external messaging.

---

## 1. Organizational Identity

**Name:** Little But Tall Charities

**Origin of the name:** Little But Tall draws from the Jamaican expression **“Likkle but Tallawah.”** The phrase reflects the spirit of Jamaica: small in size, but powerful in impact. For Little But Tall, it represents young people who may be underestimated because of their circumstances but who can make extraordinary contributions when given access to opportunity, support, and encouragement.

**Core belief:**

> **Potential is everywhere. Opportunity is not.**

This belief anchors the organization’s work. Little But Tall does not exist because young people lack ability. It exists because many young people lack consistent access to opportunity, exposure, relationships, and resources that help them discover and pursue their potential.

---

## 2. Mission

**Mission statement:**

> Little But Tall expands opportunities for young people in underserved communities by connecting them with mentorship, education, resources, and experiences that help them build confidence, discover their potential, and create lasting impact in their communities and beyond.

**Short mission language:**

> Little But Tall helps young people in underserved communities access mentorship, education, exposure, and practical support so they can discover their potential and stand tall.

---

## 3. Vision

**Vision statement:**

> Our vision is a world where every young person, regardless of where they begin, has access to the opportunities, relationships, and encouragement they need to stand tall and make a meaningful impact.

---

## 4. Positioning

Little But Tall can be understood as an **Opportunity Development Organization**.

This means Little But Tall is not only a tutoring organization, mentoring organization, technology program, or scholarship effort. Those may be methods the organization uses. The larger purpose is to develop and expand opportunities that help young people recognize their potential, prepare for the future, and make an impact.

**Positioning statement:**

> Little But Tall is a nonprofit organization dedicated to developing opportunities that help young people discover their potential, build confidence, and create lasting impact in their communities.

## 7. Theory of Change

> When young people are exposed to new possibilities, supported by caring mentors, equipped with practical resources, and encouraged to believe in their own potential, they are more likely to build confidence, pursue education and career pathways, and create meaningful impact in their communities.

### Theory of change flow

1. **Youth encounter expanded possibilities** through exposure, programming, speakers, mentors, technology, leadership experiences, and community support.
2. **Youth receive consistent encouragement** from mentors, volunteers, families, schools, and partners.
3. **Youth gain practical resources and guidance** that reduce barriers to learning, participation, and opportunity.
4. **Youth build confidence and future awareness** by seeing pathways they may not have known were available.
5. **Youth pursue stronger next steps** in education, leadership, service, career exploration, and personal growth.
6. **Youth create impact** in their own lives, families, communities, and eventually for the next generation.

## 8. C.L.I.M.B. Framework

C.L.I.M.B. is the core growth framework behind Little But Tall. It gives young people a simple, memorable way to think about personal growth and future readiness.

| Letter | Meaning | Organizational interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| **C** | **Choose** | Young people begin by recognizing they have agency and can choose a path forward. |
| **L** | **Learn** | Youth learn from mentors, teachers, peers, experiences, and their own reflection. |
| **I** | **Intend** | Students set goals, outline next steps, and begin turning hopes into plans. |
| **M** | **Move** | Youth take action, practice perseverance, and continue even when progress is difficult. |
| **B** | **Believe** | Students build confidence in their gifts, growth, and capacity to make an impact. |

C.L.I.M.B. should remain the organization’s internal and external framework for youth development, volunteer training, program design, and storytelling.

## 9. Program Model

Little But Tall’s programs should be organized as connected expressions of one mission rather than separate unrelated projects.

### A. C.L.I.M.B.

**Role:** Core youth development framework  
**Purpose:** Help young people build confidence, make positive choices, learn from mentors, set goals, take action, and believe in what is possible.

### B. Leadership In The Community

**Role:** Local outreach and relationship-centered service  
**Purpose:** Meet youth where they are through volunteer support, community-based programs, encouragement, and practical care.

### C. Future Readiness / Next Now / AI & Life Skills

**Role:** Exposure-based future readiness  
**Purpose:** Help students see themselves as capable participants in a changing world by introducing age-appropriate ideas about technology, innovation, communication, leadership, and career possibilities.

### D. Internships & College Scholarships

**Role:** Educational and career pathway support  
**Purpose:** Connect youth with next-step opportunities, scholarships, internships, career exposure, and preparation for education or work.

### E. Opportunity Abroad

**Role:** International educational opportunity support  
**Purpose:** Expand the mission beyond local borders by helping students access uniforms, transportation, meals, school supplies, technology, camps, and other practical supports that remove barriers to education.

## 11. Student Outcomes

Little But Tall should track outcomes that reflect the real purpose of the organization, not only activity counts.

### Immediate outcomes

- Students feel seen, encouraged, and supported.
- Students are exposed to new careers, pathways, people, and possibilities.
- Students understand the C.L.I.M.B. framework and can apply it to their own goals.
- Students participate in programs, sessions, events, or support opportunities.

### Intermediate outcomes

- Students show increased confidence and future awareness.
- Students can identify at least one new educational, career, or leadership pathway.
- Students build positive relationships with mentors, volunteers, or community partners.
- Students take practical next steps toward school, career, service, or personal goals.

### Long-term outcomes

- Students pursue education, training, leadership, service, or career opportunities they may not have previously considered.
- Students become more prepared to navigate adulthood and contribute to their communities.
- Students eventually return as mentors, leaders, donors, or advocates for the next generation.
